Making maps, making money - how supply chain mapping improves your business

Cartographers use their skills to create the documents that attempt to model the real world as closely as possible. In product supply chains we have a slightly different, but related, problem. Improve supplier response performance by asking the right questions

Thu, Jul 7, 2016 1:30 PM - 2:00 PM BST

Suppliers can be inundated with questions and requests for information from brands - not only about the products they sell, but also about the processes undertaken by their suppliers further upstream. However, they might not know any more about who is involved in the supply chain than than you do. Reluctance to share confidential information about who their customers is another barrier to supplier engagement.

As a brand, asking the right questions to the right organisations in the supply chain is crucial to getting the information you need. Join our webinar to learn how String3 is easy for your suppliers to use. By protecting their identity and the identities of those they source from, as well as making it easy for them to answer questions, improves their responsiveness. This gives you more accurate and more timely data about your product supply chains.

All supply chains contain risk, how exposed are you?

Thu, Aug 18, 2016 1:00 PM - 1:30 PM BST

Regulatory and reputational risks are rife within product supply chains. Deforestation, modern slavery, animal welfare, chemical usage… How do you know where to start? It's possible to say with certainty that modern slavery exists - but is it in your present in the supply chains that made your products? Deforestation unquestionably happens, but are your products part of the problem? Are you going to ask the 1000+ organisations involved, from raw material onwards to try and find out? Even if you knew who they were? Join our webinar to learn how to use String3 to take a risk based approach to managing the risks within your product supply chains.
